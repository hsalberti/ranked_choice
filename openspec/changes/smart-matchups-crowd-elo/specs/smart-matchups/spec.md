## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Fixed Round-1 opener
The system SHALL present `vance` versus `newsom` as the first matchup of every fresh Tier-1 session, regardless of any prior state. Card-slot assignment (which is shown on the left vs. the right) MAY be randomized.

#### Scenario: First matchup is always Vance vs. Newsom
- **WHEN** a user starts a fresh ballot
- **THEN** the first matchup presented contains exactly the two candidate IDs `vance` and `newsom`

#### Scenario: Card sides may swap
- **WHEN** the same user starts two fresh ballots in different sessions
- **THEN** Vance may appear on the left in one and on the right in the other

### Requirement: Round-2 hand-picked rival map
The system SHALL maintain a constant map `R2_RIVAL` exported from `candidates.js` with exactly two entries: `vance → rubio` and `newsom → aoc`. When the Round-1 winner has an entry in `R2_RIVAL`, Round 2 SHALL pit the winner against the mapped rival. When the Round-1 winner has no entry, Round 2 SHALL fall back to an adaptive selection (no hand-picked rival).

#### Scenario: Vance wins R1 → R2 is Vance vs. Rubio
- **WHEN** the user picks `vance` in Round 1
- **THEN** Round 2 presents `vance` versus `rubio`

#### Scenario: Newsom wins R1 → R2 is Newsom vs. AOC
- **WHEN** the user picks `newsom` in Round 1
- **THEN** Round 2 presents `newsom` versus `aoc`

#### Scenario: R2_RIVAL has no other entries
- **WHEN** code or tests inspect `R2_RIVAL`
- **THEN** the map contains exactly the keys `vance` and `newsom` and no others

### Requirement: Adaptive matchup selection from Round 3 onward
From Round 3 of Tier 1 onward, and across all rounds of Tier 2 and Tier 3, the system SHALL select each next matchup by the following mix: 70% probability "close-rated pair" (two candidates in the active tier whose Glicko ratings are nearest among unused or under-used pairs), 30% probability "random pair" (uniform over allowed pairs). The active tier's candidates are the only ones eligible.

#### Scenario: Adaptive mix in long runs
- **WHEN** simulated runs of 10000 Tier-1 ballots are sampled
- **THEN** the fraction of post-R2 matchups generated by the "close-rated" branch is within ±3 percentage points of 0.70

#### Scenario: Same tier only
- **WHEN** the engine selects a matchup during Tier 2
- **THEN** both candidates in the matchup have `tier === 2`

### Requirement: Coverage floor before any repeats
Within a single tier, the system SHALL ensure every candidate in the active tier appears in at least one matchup before any candidate appears in a second matchup.

#### Scenario: No early repeats in Tier 1
- **WHEN** Tier 1 reaches matchup number 8 of 15 candidates
- **THEN** at least 8 distinct candidates have appeared at least once
- **AND** no candidate has appeared three or more times

#### Scenario: Coverage floor satisfied before adaptive can repeat
- **WHEN** the adaptive selector would pick a pair that would cause a candidate's third appearance while some Tier-1 candidate has zero appearances
- **THEN** the selector replaces one slot of the pair with an unseen candidate

### Requirement: Glicko-2 stop condition
The system SHALL implement Glicko-2 (τ=0.5, RD₀=350, σ₀=0.06, rating₀=1500) for all rating updates. Tier 1 SHALL terminate when either (a) the candidates ranked 1 through 5 by rating have pairwise non-overlapping 90% confidence intervals (rating ± 1.645 × RD), OR (b) the user has cast 18 Tier-1 votes, whichever happens first. A minimum floor of 10 Tier-1 votes SHALL be enforced before condition (a) is evaluated.

#### Scenario: Stop on confidence separation
- **WHEN** after vote 14, the top-5 candidates by rating have pairwise non-overlapping 90% CIs
- **THEN** the engine ends Tier 1 and advances to the results screen

#### Scenario: Stop on vote cap
- **WHEN** the user has cast 18 Tier-1 votes and the top-5 CIs still overlap
- **THEN** the engine still ends Tier 1 and advances to the results screen

#### Scenario: Minimum floor prevents premature stop
- **WHEN** after vote 6, top-5 CIs already appear pairwise non-overlapping
- **THEN** the engine continues voting and re-evaluates the stop condition at vote 11

### Requirement: Stop condition for opt-in tiers
The system SHALL apply the same Glicko-2 stop condition logic to Tier 2 and Tier 3, parameterized by tier size: Tier 2 minimum floor of 6 votes and cap of 12 votes; Tier 3 minimum floor of 8 votes and cap of 15 votes.

#### Scenario: Tier 2 caps at 12 votes
- **WHEN** the user has cast 12 Tier-2 votes
- **THEN** the engine ends Tier 2 regardless of CI separation

### Requirement: Dynamic-opener feature flag (off in v1)
The system SHALL include a `DYNAMIC_OPENER` boolean feature flag, default `false`. When `false` the fixed `vance` vs. `newsom` opener applies. When `true`, the opener instead selects the top-2 candidates by global crowd ELO for the visitor's country (falling back to global if local n is below the min-N threshold). The flag MUST remain `false` for the v1 release of this change.

#### Scenario: Flag default
- **WHEN** the change ships
- **THEN** `DYNAMIC_OPENER` is `false` and the fixed opener applies
